Research Focus

Six interconnected research programmes advancing the science and medicine of hearing restoration.

Our laboratory operates at the intersection of fundamental auditory biology and translational medicine. We combine molecular approaches in model organisms with rigorous preclinical evaluation in adult mice, and collaborate directly with clinical colleagues to ensure our findings are grounded in patient need. The six programmes below reflect the breadth and coherence of this integrated strategy.

Hair Cell Regeneration

We investigate the molecular mechanisms and signalling pathways that enable non-mammalian vertebrates — birds, fish, amphibians — to naturally regenerate sensory hair cells after acoustic or chemical injury. By identifying key transcription factors, Wnt/Notch pathway modulators, and supporting-cell plasticity signals, we aim to unlock latent regenerative capacity in the adult mammalian cochlea and develop interventions that promote proliferative regeneration where none currently exists.

Gene Therapy Development

We engineer adeno-associated virus (AAV) and dual-vector constructs designed to deliver corrective gene payloads to inner ear hair cells and supporting cells with high specificity. A flagship programme targets otoferlin-deficient congenital deafness (DFNB9) using a split-intein dual-AAV strategy. Beyond otoferlin, we are expanding our library of gene therapy approaches to address GJB2, MYO7A, and other high-prevalence hereditary deafness loci, while optimising intracochlear delivery methods for safety and efficacy.

Drug Screening Platforms

We have established a systematic platform for high-throughput and medium-throughput phenotypic screening of small-molecule libraries in ex vivo cochlear explants and in vivo adult mouse models. The platform integrates automated confocal imaging, machine-learning-based quantification of hair cell density and morphology, and electrophysiological outcome measures. Compounds that promote hair cell survival, support cell reprogramming, or stimulate progenitor proliferation are prioritised for mechanistic follow-up and in vivo validation.

Clinical Translation

Close integration with the University Hospital Tübingen ENT clinic enables rapid translation of laboratory findings into clinical evaluation. Active translational programmes include photon-counting detector CT with deep-learning-supported denoising for dose-reduced cochlear implant preoperative planning, and multicentre protocols for perioperative standardisation in ear surgery derived from the ADANO otology working group. Each project maintains bidirectional feedback between clinical observations and bench-level investigation.

Vestibular & Phonation Research

Beyond cochlear research, we investigate balance and voice function. Our digital vestibular therapy programme is currently the subject of a confirmatory randomised controlled trial evaluating app-based vestibular rehabilitation for patients with chronic vestibular vertigo. Separately, we are developing non-contact laryngeal magnetomyography as a novel, non-invasive modality for phonation assessment, offering a practical alternative to percutaneous electromyography in the diagnosis of laryngeal movement disorders.

Translational Therapeutic Development

We coordinate end-to-end translational pipelines from target identification through preclinical proof-of-concept to IND-enabling studies. This programme integrates outputs from all five research areas above into coherent development packages suitable for academic-industry partnerships and regulatory submission. We actively foster collaborations with biotech companies, clinical trial networks, and patient advocacy groups to ensure that scientific advances reach the patients who need them with minimal delay.

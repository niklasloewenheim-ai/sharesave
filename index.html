Our Mission

Understanding the biology of hearing and translating discoveries into clinical therapies.

The Löwenheim Laboratory investigates the fundamental mechanisms of auditory hair cell biology, drawing on the remarkable regenerative capacity observed in non-mammalian vertebrates such as birds and fish. Unlike these species, adult mammals — including humans — permanently lose sensory hair cells following noise exposure, ototoxic drug treatment, or genetic insults. Our team works to understand why mammalian cochleae fail to regenerate and what molecular switches might be reactivated to reverse this deficit.

A central focus of our work is translating insights from model systems into therapeutically actionable strategies. We employ cutting-edge gene therapy vectors, small-molecule drug screening, and advanced organoid platforms to test candidate interventions in adult mice — the critical step between basic discovery and clinical development. By bridging molecular biology with clinical audiology, we accelerate the path from bench to bedside.

Our collaborative, interdisciplinary environment encompasses clinician scientists, molecular biologists, and translational researchers united by a common goal: delivering durable, safe hearing restoration to patients whose lives are profoundly affected by hearing loss. We pursue rigorous science with the urgency that patients deserve.

Model-to-Medicine

Leveraging hair cell regeneration biology from non-mammalian species to develop strategies applicable in adult mammalian cochleae.

Gene Therapy Platforms

Engineering viral and non-viral delivery systems to restore hearing gene function in patients with hereditary deafness.

Clinical Integration

Embedded within University Hospital Tübingen ENT, ensuring direct feedback between clinical observations and laboratory hypotheses.

Drug Discovery

High-throughput phenotypic screening identifies small molecules that promote hair cell survival or trigger pro-regenerative cascades.

Meet the Principal Investigator

Leading the lab with decades of experience at the intersection of clinical otology and translational neuroscience.

HL

Prof. Hubert Löwenheim, MD PhD

Principal Investigator · Department Chair

Hubert Löwenheim leads the Department of Otolaryngology — Head & Neck Surgery at University Hospital Tübingen. A physician-scientist by training, he has dedicated his career to understanding the molecular basis of sensory hair cell loss and developing gene- and cell-based hearing therapies. His dual role as practicing clinician and research director ensures that laboratory discoveries are continuously evaluated against the real-world needs of patients.

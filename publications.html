Feasibility of Dose Reduction in the Context of Preoperative Diagnostics in Cochlear Implant Surgery With a Photon-Counting Detector CT and Deep Learning-Supported Denoising
Schade-Mann T, Brendlin A, Afat S, et al.

Demonstrates that photon-counting detector CT combined with deep-learning-supported denoising can significantly reduce ionising radiation dose for cochlear implant preoperative planning while preserving diagnostic accuracy for cochlear duct length measurement — a clinically meaningful advance in patient safety for a procedure performed worldwide.

Trial
DB-OTO Gene Therapy for Inherited Deafness
Valayannopoulos V, Bance M, Carvalho DS, et al.

Reports first-in-human results of DB-OTO, a dual adeno-associated virus 1 gene therapy delivering full-length human otoferlin cDNA to cochlear inner hair cells. The trial addresses congenital, prelingual deafness caused by biallelic OTOF mutations (DFNB9) and represents a landmark in genetic hearing restoration, demonstrating measurable auditory improvements in treated participants.

Prä- und postoperative Standards in der Ohrchirurgie – interne Umfrage der ADANO Otologie-Fachgruppe
Esser-van de Loo J, Zahnert T, Rak K, et al.

A nationwide internal survey conducted by the ADANO Otology Working Group examining pre- and postoperative care standards in ear surgery across German-speaking centres. The study maps current practice variation and provides a foundation for the development of evidence-based perioperative protocols to harmonise and improve clinical outcomes.

Digital Vertigo Therapy: Study Protocol for a Confirmatory Randomized Controlled Trial in Patients With Vestibular Vertigo
Wirth M, Pieper J, Heller U, et al.

Presents the full protocol for a multicentre, confirmatory, randomised controlled trial evaluating a digital therapeutic app designed to facilitate central vestibular compensation in patients with chronic vestibular vertigo. Primary outcomes assess symptom severity and functional improvement after a structured home-based rehabilitation programme, establishing a framework for digital medicine in otology.

Phonation Differentiation by Non-Contact Laryngeal Magnetomyography
Marquetand J, Noury N, Lu H, et al.

Provides a proof-of-concept demonstration that non-contact laryngeal magnetomyography — measuring magnetic fields generated by laryngeal muscle electrical activity without skin contact or needle insertion — can reliably differentiate phonation patterns. This non-invasive modality offers a promising alternative to conventional electromyography for clinical assessment of vocal fold paralysis and laryngeal movement disorders.
